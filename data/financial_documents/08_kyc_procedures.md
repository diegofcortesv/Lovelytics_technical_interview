# Know Your Customer (KYC) Procedures

## Introduction
Know Your Customer (KYC) refers to the process financial institutions use to verify the identity of their clients and assess potential risks of illegal intentions in the business relationship.

## Objectives of KYC

### Primary Goals
- **Identity verification**: Confirm customer is who they claim to be
- **Risk assessment**: Evaluate the risk customer poses
- **Compliance**: Meet regulatory requirements
- **Fraud prevention**: Detect and prevent identity theft
- **AML/CFT**: Combat money laundering and terrorism financing

### Business Benefits
- Reduced fraud losses
- Regulatory compliance
- Enhanced reputation
- Better customer understanding
- Improved risk management

## KYC Components

### Customer Identification Program (CIP)
**Required information for individuals**:
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address
- Identification number (SSN, passport, driver's license)
- Occupation
- Email and phone number

**Required information for businesses**:
- Legal business name
- Business address
- Tax identification number
- Business structure and registration documents
- Beneficial owners (25% or greater ownership)
- Authorized signatories
- Nature of business

### Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
**Level 1: Simplified Due Diligence**
- For low-risk customers
- Basic identity verification
- Limited documentation
- Minimal ongoing monitoring

**Level 2: Standard Due Diligence**
- Identity verification with government-issued documents
- Address verification
- Understanding purpose and nature of business relationship
- Source of funds assessment
- Expected transaction patterns

**Level 3: Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)**
- For high-risk customers
- Additional documentation requirements
- Source of wealth verification
- Beneficial ownership identification
- Regular monitoring and review
- Senior management approval

### Ongoing Monitoring
- Transaction pattern analysis
- Regular information updates
- Periodic reviews (annual or based on risk)
- Alert investigation
- Changes in customer status or behavior

## High-Risk Customer Categories

### Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
**Definition**: Individuals holding prominent public positions or their close associates

**Categories**:
- Heads of state or government
- Senior politicians and officials
- Senior executives of state-owned enterprises
- Important political party officials
- Senior judicial or military officials
- Family members and close associates

**Additional requirements**:
- Senior management approval
- Source of wealth determination
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring
- Documentation of relationship rationale

### High-Risk Jurisdictions
**Characteristics**:
- FATF-identified high-risk countries
- Countries with inadequate AML/CFT controls
- Tax havens without transparency
- Countries with high corruption levels
- Sanctioned jurisdictions

### High-Risk Industries
- Money service businesses
- Casino and gaming
- Precious metals and stones
- Art dealers
- Real estate
- Cash-intensive businesses
- Virtual asset service providers

## Document Verification

### Acceptable Identity Documents
**Primary documents**:
- Passport
- Government-issued photo ID
- Driver's license
- National identity card

**Secondary documents** (for address verification):
- Utility bill (recent, within 3 months)
- Bank statement
- Government correspondence
- Lease agreement

### Verification Methods
**In-person verification**:
- Visual inspection of documents
- Comparison of photo with individual
- Check security features
- Record document details

**Remote verification**:
- Document upload and review
- Video verification (e-KYC)
- Third-party verification services
- Electronic identity verification
- Biometric verification

### Document Checks
- Validity (not expired)
- Authenticity (security features)
- Consistency (information matches across documents)
- Ownership (belongs to person presenting it)

## Regulatory Requirements

### United States
**Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)**:
- Customer Identification Program (CIP) required
- Verification of identity
- Recordkeeping requirements
- Suspicious activity monitoring

**USA PATRIOT Act**:
- Enhanced due diligence for foreign accounts
- Beneficial ownership identification
- Correspondent banking due diligence

**FinCEN Beneficial Ownership Rule**:
- Identify beneficial owners (25%+ ownership)
- Collect and verify information
- Maintain records for 5 years

### European Union
**4th and 5th Anti-Money Laundering Directives**:
- Risk-based approach
- Enhanced due diligence requirements
- Beneficial ownership registers
- PEP definitions and procedures

**GDPR Considerations**:
- Data minimization
- Purpose limitation
- Storage limitation
- Customer consent and rights

### International Standards
**FATF Recommendations**:
- Risk-based approach to CDD
- Beneficial ownership identification
- Enhanced measures for high-risk customers
- Ongoing monitoring requirements

## KYC Process Workflow

### Step 1: Customer Onboarding
1. Customer completes application
2. Provides required documentation
3. Confirms information accuracy
4. Agrees to terms and conditions

### Step 2: Verification
1. Document verification
2. Identity authentication
3. Address confirmation
4. Database checks (sanctions, PEP)
5. Adverse media screening

### Step 3: Risk Assessment
1. Assign risk rating (low, medium, high)
2. Determine due diligence level
3. Identify additional requirements
4. Obtain necessary approvals

### Step 4: Documentation
1. Record all information
2. Store documents securely
3. Create audit trail
4. Update customer profile

### Step 5: Ongoing Monitoring
1. Monitor transactions
2. Update information periodically
3. Investigate anomalies
4. Conduct periodic reviews
5. Re-assess risk ratings

## KYC Best Practices

### Program Design
1. **Risk-based approach**: Allocate resources based on risk
2. **Clear policies**: Document procedures and responsibilities
3. **Technology leverage**: Use automation where appropriate
4. **Training**: Regular education for staff
5. **Quality assurance**: Periodic reviews and audits

### Customer Communication
1. **Transparency**: Explain why information is needed
2. **Data security**: Assure customers of protection
3. **Timelines**: Set expectations for onboarding
4. **Updates**: Communicate when re-verification needed
5. **Support**: Provide help for document issues

### Documentation Standards
1. **Completeness**: Ensure all required information collected
2. **Legibility**: Clear, readable documents
3. **Consistency**: Information matches across sources
4. **Currency**: Recent documents (typically < 3 months for address)
5. **Retention**: Maintain for required period (typically 5 years)

### Monitoring Program
1. **Transaction monitoring**: Flag unusual patterns
2. **Information updates**: Request periodic refreshes
3. **Risk reassessment**: Review when triggers occur
4. **Investigation**: Promptly review alerts
5. **Escalation**: Clear process for concerning activity
