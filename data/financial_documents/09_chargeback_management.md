# Chargeback Management and Prevention

## What is a Chargeback?

A chargeback is a reversal of a credit card transaction, initiated by the cardholder through their issuing bank. It's a consumer protection mechanism that can become costly for merchants.

## Chargeback Process

### Step 1: Customer Dispute
- Cardholder contacts issuing bank
- Claims unauthorized, fraudulent, or problematic transaction
- Bank temporarily credits customer account

### Step 2: Chargeback Initiation
- Issuing bank reviews claim
- Determines validity
- Initiates chargeback if justified
- Debits merchant account

### Step 3: Merchant Response
- Merchant receives chargeback notification
- Has limited time to respond (typically 7-21 days)
- Provides compelling evidence
- Accepts chargeback or disputes

### Step 4: Arbitration (if needed)
- Card network reviews both sides
- Makes final decision
- Winner receives funds
- Loser pays arbitration fees

## Chargeback Reason Codes

### Fraud-Related (10.4, 4837, 57)
- **Card not present fraud**: CNP transactions without authorization
- **Lost or stolen card**: Legitimate card used fraudulently
- **Account takeover**: Unauthorized access to account

### Authorization Issues (4808, 4834, 11.1)
- **No authorization**: Transaction processed without approval
- **Declined authorization**: Transaction forced through after decline
- **Expired authorization**: Delay between authorization and settlement

### Processing Errors (4834, 4842, 12)
- **Duplicate processing**: Transaction charged multiple times
- **Incorrect amount**: Charged amount differs from receipt
- **Late presentment**: Transaction submitted after deadline

### Consumer Disputes (4853, 4855, 13)
- **Product not received**: Item never delivered
- **Product not as described**: Item differs from description
- **Services not provided**: Service not rendered
- **Quality issues**: Defective or damaged goods
- **Cancelled recurring**: Subscription not properly cancelled

## Chargeback Ratios and Thresholds

### Industry Standards
- **Acceptable**: < 0.65% of transactions
- **Warning level**: 0.65% - 0.9%
- **Excessive**: > 0.9%
- **High-risk**: > 1.5%

### Consequences of High Ratios
- Increased processing fees
- Rolling reserves required
- Enhanced monitoring programs
- Account termination
- Placement on TMF/MATCH list (unable to process cards)

## Financial Impact

### Direct Costs
- Transaction amount refunded
- Original processing fees forfeited
- Chargeback fee ($15-$100 per incident)
- Lost merchandise or shipping

### Indirect Costs
- Administrative time
- Customer acquisition costs
- Damage to processor relationship
- Increased processing rates
- Potential account termination

## Fraud Prevention Strategies

### Identity Verification
- **Address Verification Service (AVS)**: Match billing address
- **CVV/CVC verification**: Three-digit security code
- **Phone number validation**: Verify contact information
- **Email address checks**: Domain age and reputation
- **Device fingerprinting**: Unique device identification

### 3D Secure Authentication
- **Verified by Visa**
- **Mastercard SecureCode**
- **American Express SafeKey**
- Shifts liability to issuer for authenticated transactions

### Fraud Detection Tools
- Velocity checks (transactions per time period)
- Geolocation analysis
- Proxy/VPN detection
- High-risk country blocking
- Unusual purchase patterns
- Order value thresholds

### Manual Review
- High-value orders
- New customers
- International transactions
- Multiple payment attempts
- Mismatched billing/shipping addresses

## Documentation Requirements

### For Chargeback Disputes
**Essential evidence**:
- Copy of receipt or invoice
- Proof of delivery (signature, tracking)
- Customer communication records
- Terms and conditions acceptance
- Product/service descriptions
- Refund policy
- IP address and transaction details

**Strong supporting evidence**:
- Previous purchase history
- AVS/CVV match results
- 3D Secure authentication records
- Photos of delivered items
- Service completion documentation

### Record Retention
- Maintain for minimum 2-3 years
- Easily retrievable
- Well-organized system
- Digital preferred
- Include all transaction details

## Chargeback Representment

### Building a Strong Case
1. **Organized evidence**: Clear, relevant documentation
2. **Compelling letter**: Explain transaction legitimacy
3. **Address specific reason code**: Counter the exact claim
4. **Professional presentation**: Well-formatted documents
5. **Timely submission**: Meet all deadlines

### Win Rate Factors
- Strength of evidence
- Reason code type
- Merchant track record
- Quality of presentation
- Card brand policies

### When to Accept
- Weak evidence
- Legitimate customer complaint
- Cost exceeds transaction value
- Time investment not justified
- Maintain customer relationship
